Proposition A.1.5.

Soit \(a_k\) une suite de nombres et \(C\) une constante, alors
  1. \(\displaystyle \sum_{k=i}^{n} a_k = \sum_{l=i}^{n} a_l = \sum_{j=i}^{n} a_j = \cdots \)
  2. \(\displaystyle \sum_{k=1}^{n}C=nC\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \sum_{k=m}^nC=(n-m+1)C\)
  4. \(\displaystyle \sum_{k=1}^{n}Ca_k=C\sum_{k=1}^{n}a_k\)
  5. \(\displaystyle \sum_{k=1}^{n}\bigl(a_k+b_k\bigr)=\left(\sum_{k=1}^{n}a_k\right)+\left(\sum_{k=1}^{n}b_k\right)\)
  6. \(\displaystyle \sum_{k=1}^{n} k= \frac{n(n+1)}{2}\)
  7. \(\displaystyle \sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 = \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}\)
  8. \(\displaystyle \sum_{k=1}^{n}k^3 = \frac{n^2(n+1)^2}{4}\)
